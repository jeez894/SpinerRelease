Spiner
Danger Level: 30%
Classification: Non-biological Entity (Unstable)

“I don’t think this thing is alive.
But I’m not convinced it’s dead either.”
— Sigurd

Description

The Spiner is not a creature in the conventional sense.
Initial examinations suggest an impossible aggregation of muscle fibers and bones, assembled without any recognizable or even logical structure. These components appear misaligned, fused, and stretched, as if pulled together under twisted tension.

A thin membrane, similar to skin envelops the mass. This “skin” shows no signs of natural growth and appears to function purely as a containment layer.

No organs have been conclusively identified ouside of his eyes.

Behavior

The Spiner exhibits prolonged stalking behavior, remaining just outside direct observation. Subjects report a strong sensation of being watched well before visual contact is established.

The entity may emits sounds that vary between individuals. Analysis suggests the Spiner does not imitate distress or fear, but instead reproduces sounds the target finds deeply comforting. The mechanism appears reflexive and organic rather than deliberate.

Once contact is made, the Spiner may restrain and transport the subject.
Notably, captured individuals describe the experience as calm, controlled, and in some cases comforting. No immediate physical harm is observed during transport.

Subjects can be forcibly released if the entity sustains damage.

Notes

Repeated encounters imply the Spiner reacts unpredictably to lethal force.
Field reports consistently end after attempted termination.

DO NOT KILL.
